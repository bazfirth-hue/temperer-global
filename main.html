01 / 04

Site Vulnerability Intelligence

Most security failures are not surprises. They are gaps that were never properly mapped.

Our CNI-qualified assessors conduct structured vulnerability analysis across physical, procedural, and technological attack surfaces — producing a classified threat register and a tiered remediation roadmap delivered within agreed timescales. No generic frameworks. No theoretical models. Assessments grounded in direct operational experience at the highest levels of national security.

  • Assessments conducted across CNI sites, major event venues, and corporate HQs — UK and international
  • Every assessment delivers a classified threat register with a prioritised remediation timeline, briefed directly to your board or risk committee

What the Assessment Covers

Physical layer. Perimeter integrity, access control, CCTV coverage and blind spots, vehicle threat mitigation, hostile surveillance detection indicators.

Procedural layer. Guard force effectiveness, staff security culture, incident reporting protocols, emergency response readiness.

Technological layer. Communications resilience, electronic countermeasure gaps, CCTV analytics coverage, IT/OT boundary vulnerabilities.

Deliverables

  • Classified threat register with site-specific risk ratings
  • Prioritised remediation roadmap with cost-banded options
  • Board-level briefing document suitable for regulatory submission
  • Six or twelve-month reassessment scheduling available

3D Risk Modelling & Digital Twin

See your site as a threat actor would — before a single physical change is made.

Our LiDAR-powered digital twin capability maps your venue or facility in precise spatial detail — enabling real-time modelling of crowd flows, ingress vulnerabilities, blast radius scenarios, and evacuation bottlenecks before they become operational realities. Fully Martyn's Law audit-compliant. Deliverable within five working days for standard sites.

  • Applied to G7 Summit security planning and major national event infrastructure — the same capability, now available to you
  • Martyn's Law audit-ready documentation produced as standard. SIA-compliant output for Standard and Enhanced Tier venues

How It Works

LiDAR scanning produces a millimetre-accurate 3D model of your venue or facility. We overlay crowd-flow modelling, ingress vulnerability mapping, blast-radius scenarios, and evacuation bottleneck analysis — all within a secure digital environment before any physical change is made.

Martyn's Law Compliance

Output is structured to satisfy both Standard and Enhanced Tier SIA requirements. Delivered as part of a compliance pack suitable for submission to your SIA Registered Inspector within five working days for standard sites.

Deliverables

  • Full-fidelity digital twin of your venue or facility
  • Ingress vulnerability and crowd-flow assessment
  • Martyn's Law SIA-compliant audit documentation
  • Optional ongoing monitoring and annual refresh licence

Our Heritage

The Name

Operation Temperer is the UK government's framework for deploying military assets in direct support of civilian authorities in the event of a major terrorist attack on UK soil.

It is not a marketing concept. It is a standing order — activated when the civilian response alone is insufficient to protect the public.

Our founders operated under that mandate.

We did not choose the name to borrow authority we hadn't earned. We chose it because this consultancy was built on the same doctrine that underpins the operation itself: speed of assessment, precision of response, and the protection of life at scale — without hesitation and without error.

We are not affiliated with HM Government.
We are the people they trained.
